How to Publish with JIGG



JIGG welcomes students to submit their work from a considerable breadth of research topics, however, please carefully review the following eligibility criteria and article guidelines before submission.

Nature/Theme of Research
We aim to publish topics of specific interest which includes but are not limited to - molecular genetics, medical genetics, cancer genetics, epigenetics, immunogenetics, microbial genetics, viral genetics, genetics of infectious diseases, genome engineering, comparative genomics, functional genomics, developmental biology, regenerative and stem cell biology, computational biology and bioinformatics.

As we aim to publish articles on additional topics not listed above, please contact us should you not find your research pertinent to one of the listed topics.

Authorship
We accept submissions from students whose original work has been completed prior to, during or shortly after their undergraduate or professional degree program (e.g. BSc, BA, BScN, MD, DMD) in an accredited higher education institution irrespective of nationality.

Exclusion Criteria
We do not accept work either completely or partially completed during a graduate degree program (e.g. MSc, MASc, PhD). We do not publish articles that have been previously published, in either a undergraduate or professional academic journal. As well, we do not publish articles containing proprietary information. Lastly, please note that we do not publish articles that are not written in English; in other words, all articles written in a foreign language must be translated to written English prior to submission.

Consent
By submitting to the Journal of Innovations in Genetics and Genomics (JIGG), you must acknowledge and agree to all the terms and conditions, which are included and explicitly stated in the online submission process.
We accept original research and literature review articles. Please follow the guidelines listed for a primary research article. Otherwise, please go straight to literature review article.

  • Font type and size: Times New Roman; 12
  • Article word limit (excluding figure captions): 1500 - 6500 words
  • Title length limit: 25 words
  • Margin and paper size: 1-inch on all four corners; letter size (8.5 by 11 inches)

  • Reference style: References are each numbered, superscripted, sequentially ordered as they appear in the manuscript.

    • E.g. The field of molecular biology has advanced rapidly following the elucidation of the structure of DNA 1 and its sequencing methods.2-3

    NOT: The field of molecular biology has advanced rapidly following the elucidation of the structure of DNA (Watson and Crick, 1953) and its sequencing methods (Sanger and Coulson 1975; Sanger et al. 1977).

    Reference examples:
    1. Watson, J.D., and Crick, F. H. C. Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids: A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid. Nature 171, 737–738 (1953).
    2. Sanger, F., and Coulson, A.R. A rapid method for determining sequences in DNA by primed synthesis with DNA polymerase. J Mol Biol 94(3), 441–448 (1975).
    3. Sanger, F., Nicklen, S., and Coulson, A.R. DNA sequencing with chain-terminating inhibitors. Proc Natl Acad Sci USA 74(12), 5463–5467 (1977).

    Note: Do not cite unpublished work in your reference. List all authors in your reference unless the number of authors exceed 5 individuals - in which case, cite the first author’s last name, first name initial, et al. E.g. James, A. et al.
  • Title: An informative title that captures the main idea or result of your research.

  • Author Contributions and Academic Affiliations: List all authors that have contributed to your article, include your laboratory’s principal investigator as your last author. State all academic affiliations in the authors listed.

    • E.g. James, A.1,3, Andrews, C.2, Chen, X.1, 2. Ali, H.3, Lawdowski, Y.1,2,3.
    1. Department of Molecular Biology, University of World, City, Country, Postal Code
    2. Department of Immunology, University of World, City, Country, Postal Code
    3. Institute of Genetics and Genomics, City, Country, Postal Code

  • Abstract: In 250 words, provide a brief overview and critical findings of your article.

  • Keywords: List 5 keywords pertaining your article.
    E.g. cardiac progenitor cells (CPCs), cardiac regeneration, nkx 2.5, lineage tracing, zebrafish

  • Introduction: Provide concise background information pertaining your work and cite relevant, published articles that provided the framework or rationale for your research.

  • Methods: Describe the specific technique or assay used in your experimentation. Include the specific instrument model information and source of the reagents/model organisms, if applicable. Each method should be elaborated under a bolded subheading.

  • Results: Each result should be elaborated under a bolded subheading. Refer your results to each corresponding figure data.
    E.g. The expression levels of c-myc and c-kit were reduced post-x treatment, respectively (Figure 1a,b).

  • Discussions: Describe the significance of your findings - what do they implicate, how are they novel or how might they support/conflict with previous findings, how might they provide future directions in your research topic and what knowledge gaps remain unfilled?

  • Conclusion: Briefly capture the essence of your study and how this study might be helpful/applicable for future studies.

  • References: You can cite up to 35 references. See "Reference Style" in Step 2.1 for details.

  • Acknowledgement: Under 200 words, name the person(s) and organization(s) who have supported your work and how they might have done so (e.g. mentorship, resources).
    • All figures (i.e. graphs, images) and tables are to be compiled in one document, separated from the manuscript. They should also be ordered sequentially as they are mentioned in your manuscript. Include brief captions to your figures as well as legends, if applicable.

    You are limited to use up to 15 figures.
  • Manuscript: .pdf, .docx
  • Document containing all figures and tables
  • Figures: .jpg, .png, .tiff
    • Note: Ensure that the figure is as clear as possible.
    The article guidelines for a review article is the same as the primary article, besides the following modifications:
  • Word limit: 5000 words
  • Reference limit: 150 citations
  • Figure limit: 5
  • Do not include any figures or data that are not generated by yourself.
  • Tables, graphs, and cartoons generated by yourself are welcome.
